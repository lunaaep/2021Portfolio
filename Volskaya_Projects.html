Scratch Project

September 3 - September 11, 2019 for Computer Science Principles


The objective of the project was to create a working game on the platform called Scratch, which we could show to our class. My partner and I got very creative with ideas for our game, and decided on a simple arcade game, because we knew our limits of knowledge in coding and Scratch. I learned to use drag-and-drop code, as well as how to animate a background using code and make a sprite move on it. My partner and I switched off between coding and navigating very well, so our share of both roles was equal. A challenge we encountered had to do with repetition of actions, but we worked it out in the end. We were proud of the end result.





MIT App Inventor Project

September 23 - October 8, 2019 for Computer Science Principles


The objective of the project was to create a working app for an Android device, or an Android emulator. My partner and I were new to the MIT App Inventor, so we encountered some issues with how to customize and expand our app from the tutorial. My partner and I switched off between the roles, and both learned how difficult making an app actually is. Compound Machine Project

November 9 - December 4, 2020 for Principles of Engineering


In this project, our team was working towards the objective of building a working compound machine using household materials. We met during and outside of class to make a plan of what we needed to do before our next meeting. While a lot of the work was completed as a team, my main contribution was creating the video presentation that we would present to the class. An obstacle I faced was having to use a corrupted file from a classmate, which the video editing program would unfortunately crop. After failed attempts of re-rendering, I ended up screen recording the video which is how I was able to continue. Once the project was completed, we organized all of the work on the documentation page, where the video, as well as images of our sketches and models, is linked.







Bridge Designer Project

March 19 - April 9, 2021 for Honors Principles of Engineering


The objective of the project was to create a functioning bridge design that would be stable enough to carry a load, while facing constraints such as build cost, material cost, and individual material properties. It was interesting to get to know the bridge building software, as my partner and I were both new to it at first. This definitely brought some challenges in the beginning phases of our project. Another difficult aspect of the project was to figure out where to sacrifice stability for a lower cost. After many experiments and failed designs, my partner and I were able to come up with a cheap design for a steel bridge that could carry a truck across a waterway. My main responsibilities included explaining our final decision and defending the design in the Design Matrix, as well as describing our final product in detail in the Design Evaluation.
